Vetal, also known as Baital or Betal, is a mythical and supernatural being in Hindu folklore. In the context of the "Vikram and Vetal" stories, Vetal is a celestial spirit or demon who possesses the power to inhabit and control dead bodies. This mystical being is known for its cunning and mischievous nature, often challenging the intelligence and wisdom of King Vikram.

The central theme of the stories involves Vetal being captured by King Vikram, but Vetal poses a condition: he will accompany Vikram silently, but if the king utters a word during their journey, Vetal will escape. Throughout their journey, Vetal narrates moral and philosophical stories to King Vikram, each ending with a question or dilemma. King Vikram, despite facing numerous challenges and temptations, remains steadfast in his silence until the end of each story.

The tales of Vikram and Vetal are not only entertaining but also convey moral lessons and explore ethical dilemmas, making them a significant part of Indian folklore and literature. The clever and insightful conversations between Vikram and Vetal contribute to the enduring appeal of these stories.
